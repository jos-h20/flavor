What I stand for

  • Simplicity

    Not minimal for aesthetic reasons. Minimal because every unnecessary feature is a cost your users pay.

  • Privacy

    Your users didn't agree to be the product. I build as if their data is none of my business — because it isn't.

  • Craft

    The part you can't see is usually what matters most. Good architecture doesn't announce itself.

  • Honesty

    If something's going to take longer, I'll tell you before it does. If your idea has a flaw, I'll say that too.

  • Ownership

    You own everything we build together — code, IP, all of it. No lock-in, no licensing surprises, no strings.

  • Restraint

    Adding features is easy. Knowing which ones not to build is the skill that separates good software from great software.
